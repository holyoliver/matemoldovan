Video showcase

Non-Linear rescoring for a gameplay video of Tomb Raider

The video above showcases the non-linear compositional techniques used for game audio. Non-linear means, that the music/sound is not fixed to screen (or time), it can be programmed or triggered when the sound is necessary. This technique gives a lot of freedom for sound programming in games and it also saves processor power as the ‘ loops’ and ‘ one shots’ can be used in the game multiple times in different order.

Sound Design in Unreal engine


In this video I demonstrate my sound design capabilities and the implementation of sound effects in Unreal Engine 4. All sounds were either recorded (foley) or synth generated by myself. For repeating sounds (gunshot, reload), I broke down the actual sample to multiple pieces, so they can be randomised every time differently to avoid repetition.

Linear rescoring for Rayman 3 cutscene

This is my rescoring for the opening scene of a well known game, Rayman 3. I used the 'Mickey-mousing' composition technique to mimic every movement on screen with the music. (Apologies for the quality of the video, unfortunately there was no better available online.)

Linear rescoring for a gameplay video of Journey

A slightly different approach compared to the original soundtrack, I tried to find the right style which is still coherent to the game’s aesthetic. The core idea of the music is to be ‘ loopable’, so the music will still be enjoyable regardless of the time spent on the level.
