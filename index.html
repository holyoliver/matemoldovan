Video showcase

Non-Linear rescoring for a gameplay video of Tomb Raider

In this video I demonstrate my capabilities of writing non-linear music for games. Non-linear means, that the music / sound in not fixed to screen, it can be programmed or triggered when the sound actually needed. This technique gives a lot more freedom for sound programming in games, and it also saves a big amount of data of processor power (therefore more space left for graphics). (Apologies for the additional delay caused by the internal video-sound recording application.)

Sound Design in Unreal engine

In this video I demonstrate my sound design capabilities and implementing my sound effects in Unreal Engine 4. All sounds were either recorded (foley) or synth generated by myself. For repeating sounds (gunshot, reload), I broke down the actual sample to multiple pieces, so they can be randomised every time differently to avoid repetition.

Linear rescoring for Rayman 3 cutscene

This is my rescoring for the opening scene of a well known game, Rayman 3. I used the 'Mickey-mousing' compositional technique to mimic every movement on screen with the music. (Apologies for the quality of the video, unfortunately there was no better available online.)
